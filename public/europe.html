Frequently Asked Questions About Europe Tours

What is the most popular Europe tour route?
The "Classic Circuit" — Rome → Venice → Munich → Rhine Valley → Amsterdam → Paris → Swiss Alps — is the most booked route. It covers 7 countries in 10–14 days and appears in 7 of the 10 most-reviewed multi-country Europe tours. The top example, Europe Escape by Expat Explore, has 867 verified reviews and a 4.5-star overall rating. Paris, Amsterdam, and Venice appear on 100% of these top itineraries.
How much does a Europe tour cost?
Budget youth tours (18–35) start from $1,200 for 7 days. The most popular bracket is $2,500–$3,800 for a 10–14 day comfort tour. Premium guided tours run $3,800–$7,000. Luxury river cruises range from $3,000 to $15,000+. Price per day: budget $140–225, comfort $200–270, premium $270+. Eastern Europe routes are 30–40% cheaper than Western Europe.
Which tour operators have the best reviews?
Based on 280+ tours and 8,500+ reviews: Cosmos leads with 4.9★ overall. Avalon Waterways scores 4.8★ with the best food ratings (4.7★) across all operators. Contiki and Trafalgar both score 4.7★ with near-perfect guide ratings. Collette and Expat Explore score 4.5★, with Expat Explore holding the highest single-tour review volume (867 reviews). See the full 8-operator comparison table above.
How many days do you need for a Europe tour?
Analysis of 280+ tours shows 10–14 days is the sweet spot — these tours receive the most reviews per tour. Quick highlights run 7–10 days (4–7 countries). Deep exploration tours span 18–22 days (9–12 countries). The Europe Explorer by Expat Explore covers 9 countries in 18 days across France, Spain, Italy, and more.
What do travelers rate highest on Europe tours?
Guides score highest at 4.8★ average across 8,500+ reviews, with Contiki and Intrepid guides consistently hitting a perfect 5.0★. Transport scores 4.7★. Itineraries average 4.6★. Accommodation averages 4.1★. Food scores lowest at 4.0★ — experienced travelers recommend supplementing group meals with local restaurants, especially in Italy and France.
Are Europe group tours good for solo travelers?
Yes. All top operators (Expat Explore, Contiki, Trafalgar, Collette) offer both private and shared room options. Youth-focused operators like Contiki (18–35) specialize in social group dynamics and score 4.7★ overall. Solo travelers consistently rate the guide experience 4.8★+. Budget tip: shared room options can save $500–$1,500 on a 10–14 day tour.
What is the cheapest way to tour Europe?
Budget youth tours from Contiki and G Adventures start at $1,200–$1,800 for 7–12 days with hotel-style accommodation. Contiki's European Discovery (12 days, 8 countries) starts at $1,725 with 11 breakfasts and 3 dinners included. Eastern Europe itineraries (Budapest → Prague → Krakow → Berlin) run 30–40% cheaper than Western Europe routes. Book during shoulder season (March–May, September–October) for 15–25% lower prices.
What is the best time to visit Europe?
May–June and September–October offer the best balance. Tour departure data shows 85% of available departures concentrate between March and October. Summer has the most options but highest prices and biggest crowds. Winter is cheapest but many tours don't operate December–February.
What should I pack for a Europe tour?
Based on tour itineraries: comfortable walking shoes (cities require 10,000+ steps/day), layers for variable weather, a day pack, universal power adapter (Europe uses Type C/F/G/J outlets depending on country), and smart casual evening wear for group dinners. Most coaches have limited luggage space — one suitcase plus a day bag.
Do I need a visa for a Europe tour?
US, Canadian, Australian, and UK citizens can visit the 27 Schengen Area countries visa-free for up to 90 days. Starting 2026, the EU's ETIAS travel authorization is required (quick online application, valid 3 years). Non-Schengen countries on tours (UK, Croatia before 2023) may have separate requirements. Always check 6 months before departure.
